Frequently asked

The questions streamers ask first.

Everything you need to know about how drops work — for streamers and viewers.

Viewers sign up via the on-stream link or QR code. They connect their Twitch account and add a Steam trade URL to their Dropzona profile once, then they’re added to that stream’s winner pool and eligible the next time a trigger fires. No payment, no extra app to install.

No. Drops are free to enter. There are no tokens, credits, entry fees, or paywalls.

When a trigger fires and a winner is picked, Dropzona builds the Steam trade offer to that winner automatically using Steam’s official trade flow. You never have to draw a winner or send a trade by hand.

Two things: a Twitch account (for chat and OAuth) and CS2 Game State Integration enabled via a small config file in your CS2 cfg folder. Skins are purchased from your Dropzona balance and sent directly to the winner’s Steam trade URL — you don’t need to connect a Steam account on the streamer side. The in-app wizard walks through each step.

Live now: CS2 GSI event triggers, winner selection from your live signup pool, and automatic Steam trade-offer delivery. Coming soon: Test Mode, multi-language announcements, expanded drop-history and analytics, the watch-time points program, and subscriber/loyalty rules. We label every roadmap item as “Soon” on this page.

Trade offers can fail for reasons outside our control — for example, the winner has a missing or private Steam trade URL, their inventory is full, or Steam declines the offer. Dropzona surfaces the failure in your dashboard so you can resend or re-roll the winner. We don’t lose your prize.
